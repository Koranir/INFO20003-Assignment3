Stephen Edgar

About the Author

Stephen Edgar was born in Sydney in 1951. He spent the early 1970s in London before moving to Hobart, where he lived for some thirty years. Since 2005 he has lived in Sydney. He is married to the poet Judith Beveridge. He has published ten previous collections of poetry in Australia, the three most recent of which were shortlisted for the Prime Minister’s Literary Awards, Transparencies in 2018, Exhibits of the Sun in 2015, and Eldershaw in 2014 (also joint winner of the Colin Roderick Award and shortlisted for the Queensland Literary Awards). His other collections are Queuing for the Mudd Club (1985), Ancient Music (1988), Corrupted Treasures (1995), Where the Trees Were (1999), Lost in the Foreground (2003), Other Summers (2006) and History of the Day (2009). In 2012 a volume of new and selected poems, The Red Sea, was published in the United States where he has a dedicated following.
