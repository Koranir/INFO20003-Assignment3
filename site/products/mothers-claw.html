Mother’s Claw

Louise Davenport

Description

Louise Davenport ’ s short fiction is spare in the classic modernist manner. Strong underlying emotion is controlled, oblique or symbolic. The ‘ claw ’ in the title sequence is a traditional image that is strikingly re-invented through the agency of a moving statue, itself an image re-energised from the canon. In many ways her first collection is a fractured mirror, presenting key images recurrently. Or it can be read as a mosaic autobiography, the author ’ s facing the childhood horror of her mother ’ s suicide and dealing with its emotional ramifications in her adult life. Mother ’ s Claw is both cool and healing.

About the Author

Louise Davenport was born in Melbourne in 1955. Her childhood was partly spent in the Bendigo Babies’ Home and the Andrew Kerr Memorial Home for Children while her mother was receiving psychiatric treatment.

Reviews

artStreams Review

ArtStreams Like a fracturing mirror, Davenport’s short fiction presents key images recurrently. Beginning with childhood memories of the horror of her mother’s death, Davenport moves gently between stoires of her life. These stories are all coloured by the claw - the image of her mother that haunts her life. Moving roughly along a timeline, her scattered images weave a surprisingly clear story. To go to the heart of the truth is too much pain. I enter from an angle and light up the edges of my object with pastel relief. Davenport’s story is ghostly and quiet, fragments of dreams and memories entangle to produce an engaging fiction.
